Impact Training & Volunteering App - 300 Blankets

Do you want to help people in your community who experience or are at risk of homelessness but you don’t know where to start and think you lack the skills to volunteer?

Our group of 5 UX/UI Designers, discovered a not-for-profit organisation called 300 Blankets which we chose as our redesign project. We quickly realised that there was a larger opportunity to create an app for future volunteers to get involved, whilst offering educational modules to gain essential skills to apply throughout their work in the community.

Project Overview

Role

UX/UI Designer

Time

3 weeks

Tools

Figma, Miro, GitHub, Google Drive

Challenge

Design a training & volunteering app for 300 Blankets offering an engaging & intuative user experience to register & schedule time to help in the community.

Solution

  • Make volunteer’s sign-up process seamless
  • Simplify scheduling of availabilities
  • Facilitate connection & communication with other helpers
  • Create in-app training modules to help volunteers gain essential skills & confidence
  • Receive latest events & updates
  • Provide opportunity for volunteers to invite others to events

Deliverables

  • User Interviews
  • Heuristic Evaluation
  • Problem Statement
  • Proto Persona
  • Storyboard
  • Competitor Analysis
  • Feature Prioritisation Matrix
  • Card Sorting & Navigation System
  • User Flow
  • Style Guide
  • User Testing & Iterations
  • Hi-Fi Wireframes Desktop & Mobile
  • Website Development

Process

The following Design Process was applied throughout our Project:

  • Research  
  • → Define
  • → Ideate
  • → Prototype
  • → Test

Research

We conducted a Heuristic Evaluation and redlining, followed by user interviews.

Heuristic Evaluation

At first the 300 Blankets site presented with a clear layout & structure but we soon discovered many inconsistencies with buttons & patterns.

Another major problem with the site was that the white & orange colours failed WCAG AAA and AA accessibility standards.

We recieved comments that the site was lacking style & flow and felt a bit outdated, potentially leading to users not gaining an enjoyable, or informative experience.

User Testing

We wanted to understand who our users were, their experience with volunteering & engaging with not-for-profits and their thoughts on what could be improved with the connection between nfps and potential future volunteers.

Through our reserach, we wanted to find out:


  • What our user looks like?
  • What motivates someone to engage with a cause they believe in?
  • How aware are people of not-for-profit organisations?
  • What will assist in bringing greater awareness?
  • Engagement with not-for-profits & in what ways?
  • Awareness of homelessness rates in Australia
  • Ways homelessness can be helped?

Here are some insights from the interviews:

I am aware of the issue of homelessness and would like to get involved to make a difference in people's lives, but it's not always clear to me on how I can contribute.

I think connecting with others is a great idea, but I would need to learn ways on how to communicate effectively with people before I feel confident to approach anyone on the streets.

Having empathy and being aware of your surroundings is the most important starting point when deciding to help, but I also fear that more funding is needed to make real change.

Competitor Analysis

Next, we conducted a Competitor Analysis of similar sites. Our direct competitors were The Homeless project & Anonymous X and our indirect competitors were Street Smart & Homelessness Australia.

Some of our key findings were:

  • All responsive design & easily available online donation option
  • Unclear website layout
  • Repetitive information
  • Lenghthly volunteering sign-up

Define

Next we defined a typical 300 Blanktes user outlined below.

Proto Persona

Gabriel is a married sales rep from Melbourne Victoria, who enjoys the thought of volunteering and making more of an impact with causes he believes in, particularly homelessness.

Unfortunately, he does not feel confident in the skills he possesses and is unsure how to approach people while volunteering. He is also looking to be more aware of upcoming events from nfps he is engaged with, and feels being more aware would lead to him saving his own time by scheduling time where he can engage and volunteer.

Problem Statement

By analysing and synthesising our collected data, we asked ourselves the following question:

How might we raise awareness of the issue of homelessness, educate people on communication and engagement skills, and make it easy for people to get involved with a flexible volunteer calendar?

Ideate

With all of this user research and insights, we felt it came down to the importance of:

  • Training
  • Volunteering
  • Connecting

This motivated us to create the ‘IMPACT’ App which we will explain in detail below.

Information Archeticture

After conducting a user scenario test on the website, we discovered that the user finds it difficult to navigate through the website because of its complicated structure and repetative information.

Our first goal was to simplify information for the user. For this we listed all information at one place and categorised similar information under the same heading and eliminated things that were repetative.

User Flow

With a simplified version of the information architecture we created a user flow for the user to be able to navigate through the Homepage of the website to download the IMPACT app and sign up to volunteer. We conducted a feature analysis with the following areas standing out.

Four key features emerged within the IMPACT app:

  • Volunteer/Calendar function to checkout and register for upcoming events
  • Training function to gain information via quiz
  • Profile function to update any new user information
  • Chat function to connect with other volunteers.

Prototype

Then we moved on to the prototype phase of our project. We applied the follwoing style guide to create the first low-mid fidelity wireframes.

UI Style Guide

We chose a different colour scheme to the original website, where we opted for warm & welcoming colours but at the same time neutral for the app.

We arribed at a combination of warm red, blanket brown, calm earth & ocean blue that complemented each other. This colour scheme was acyually inspired from the colours of a real blanket.

In terms of typoraphy we used a combination of the Inter and Sintony styles where Inter was for headings and Sintony for paragraphs.

Lo-Fi Wireframes

In our low-mid fidelity wireframes we wanted to determine:

  • structure of the app & website
  • user flow & navigation between various screens
  • strategic placement of interactive components & buttons.

LoFi User Testing & Iterations

Following the development of our low-mid fidelity prototype, we held user tests of 6 easy tasks, where we tested basic functions and navigation of the app (account creation, events registration, training, chat & profile updates).

We received important feedback from users:

  • Add a home button to bottom nav + make all same size
  • Insert navigation icons & labels (a/b testing)
  • Create back buttons on each page
  • Adjust wording to content e.g. reword ‘exit’ to ‘cancel’
  • Remove calendar & chat landing pages
  • Move the profile icon to top right
  • Display courses on top of training section
  • Create a sequential volunteer sign up form with progress bar
  • Adjust ‘change profile photo’ flow

HiFi User Testing & Iterations

After further iterations, we conducted the same user test for our high fidelity prototype. We wanted to see whether we had improved on the navigation, layout and communication throughout the app, as well as other issues previously identified.

We incorporated valuable insights from our user testing:

  • Include skip option for app tour
  • Highlight features
  • Remove SSO (single sign on) benefits page + add Apple ID
  • Insert optional field on volunteer’s form + display all steps on top
  • Add ‘my events’ on the volunteer’s calendar + plus an invite option
  • Create different badges
  • Align button sizes + button order
  • Adjust quiz flow
  • Simplify chat connections
  • Option to change profile photo
  • Add help section with contacts

Final Thoughts & Opportunities

As we kept moving with the project, we saw an opportunity to really expand the offerings of the app, particularly in the training section. We also believed there would be room for watch support with customized notifications for events. Potentially even an Impact watch app.

Next steps:

  • Adding content within the training section, including support for video material
  • Giving users the ability to set up chat groups, check for immediate availability for events
  • Showing other 300 Blankets events to the calendar e.g. Soul Kitchen, music nights etc.
  • Watch support with upcoming events alerts, nearby events, ping location & register on the go

This project was really enjoyable for all of us, and a great learning experience particularly in how to create a multi modal offering and sell a product across both techniques.
